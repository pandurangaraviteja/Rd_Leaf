Quick guide
Read the work in four steps
This section turns the technical pipeline into a simple reader journey.
1

Problem

Field potato images are imbalanced and standard augmentation ignores disease progression over time.

2

Idea

Use Fisher–KPP reaction–diffusion to create ordered lesion progression masks offline.

3

Training

Composite lesions onto healthy leaves and train ordinary classifiers with cross-entropy only.

4

Evidence

A2s improves accuracy, calibration, transfer, and Grad-CAM localization under matched budgets.

Research claims
What the paper claims — and what it does not claim

✓ Best evaluated method

A2s gives the highest metrics among tested baselines under the reported settings.

✓ Ordering helps

A2s exceeds shuffled KPP A3, suggesting temporal ordering adds useful signal.

✓ Physics-inspired

The PDE is solved offline; no PDE residual is placed inside the training loss.

⚠ Not photorealistic

Alpha blending is structural, not a complete biological rendering of lesion evolution.

⚠ Not universal yet

Transfer was evaluated one-way; multi-region external validation remains future work.

Interpretation guardrails

The results support the utility of temporal ordering, but they do not prove that a curriculum mechanism is the only causal explanation. Batch-statistics effects, optimization trajectory effects, and implicit feature regularization remain possible.

  • A2s vs A3 isolates ordering while preserving KPP image family.
  • A2s vs A6 compares physics-structured augmentation with RandAugment.
  • Holm-Bonferroni correction keeps the reported tests significant.

rd_aug.py — Fisher–KPP PDE solver and lesion generator
# Core Fisher–KPP update
def euler_step(C, D=0.25, r=1.5, K=1.0, dt=0.05):
    L = laplacian_reflect(C)  # Neumann boundary condition
    dC = dt * (D * L + r * C * (1.0 - C / K))
    return np.clip(C + dC, 0.0, K)

for frame in range(T):
    for step in range(Nsub):
        C = euler_step(C, D, r, K, dt)
    frames.append(normalize_to_uint8(C))
training_loop.py — ordered synthetic DataLoader
# A2/A2s preserve synthetic temporal order; A3 destroys it
real_loader  = DataLoader(real_dataset,  shuffle=True,  batch_size=32)
synth_loader = DataLoader(synth_dataset, shuffle=False, batch_size=32)

for (br, yr), (bs, ys) in zip(real_loader, synth_loader):
    x = torch.cat([br, bs])
    y = torch.cat([yr, ys])
    loss = cross_entropy(model(x), y)
    loss.backward(); optimizer.step()
